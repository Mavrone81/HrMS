2. Executive Summary
This Product Requirements Document (PRD) defines the complete feature set, workflows, acceptance criteria, and compliance obligations for EzyHRM – a cloud-native Human Resource Management System purpose-built for Singapore organisations. The system covers the full employee lifecycle from recruitment to offboarding and is designed for scalability across SMEs, large enterprises, government bodies, and non-profit organisations.
EzyHRM operates in full compliance with the Employment Act (Cap. 91), CPF Act (Cap. 36), Personal Data Protection Act 2012, IRAS Income Tax Act (IR8A/IR21/AIS), Skills Development Levy Act, Employment of Foreign Manpower Act, Child Development Co-Savings Act, and Ministry of Manpower digital submission requirements including CPF e-Submit@cpf and MyCareersFuture.
2.1 Product Vision
To be Singapore’s most compliant, configurable, and user-friendly HRMS – eliminating manual HR administration, ensuring zero-defect statutory compliance, and providing workforce insights that support data-driven people management.

4. Module 1: Payroll & CPF Management
The payroll module is the core engine of EzyHRM. It processes all compensation types in full compliance with the Employment Act (Cap. 91), CPF Act (Cap. 36), Income Tax Act, and Skills Development Levy Act. All formulas, contribution rates, ceilings, and workflows below are applied automatically without manual calculation.
4.1 Payroll Run Types & Workflow
Monthly Run: Processed once per month for all employees. Payroll Officer initiates run for selected period and employee group. System locks attendance, leave, and claims data for the period, computes gross pay, deductions, and net pay per employee. Payroll Officer reviews exception and variance report. Maker submits for Checker approval (must be different user). Checker approves. System generates payslips, bank GIRO file, CPF submission file, SDL file, and accounting journal entries. Payroll is finalised and locked; subsequent changes require an ad-hoc correction run with full audit trail.
Bi-Monthly Run: Processed twice per month (e.g., 1st and 15th), common for weekly-rated workers and part-timers.
Ad-Hoc / Off-Cycle Run: Used for terminations, corrections, and supplementary payments (bonus, AWS). Each ad-hoc run requires maker-checker approval and is logged in audit trail.
Payslip Timing: Payslips must be issued by the date of payment if payment is by bank transfer, or within 3 working days after payment if paid in person or by cheque, as required by MOM.
4.2 Gross Pay Computation
Basic Salary: Monthly Basic = Contracted Annual Salary ÷ 12.
Daily Rate: Monthly Basic ÷ 26 (Employment Act standard working days for deduction/encashment purposes).
Hourly Rate: Daily Rate ÷ Normal Daily Working Hours.
Gross Pay Formula: Gross Pay = Basic Salary + Fixed Allowances + Variable Allowances + Overtime Pay + Shift Differentials + Commission + Bonus/AWS + Government-Paid Leave Reimbursements − No-Pay Leave Deductions − Absence Deductions.
4.3 Overtime Computation (Employment Act Part IV Employees)
Hourly Basic Rate: = (Monthly Basic Salary × 12) ÷ (52 × Contracted Weekly Hours). Allowances excluded from OT rate base unless contractually included.
OT Pay: = Hourly Basic Rate × 1.5 × OT Hours worked (OT rate can be adjusted by HR Manager to increase or decrease).
Monthly OT Cap: 72 hours per month per Employment Act s.38(5). System auto-enforces cap; flags and alerts HR when approaching cap; stops OT accrual at cap unless HR overrides with documented justification in audit trail.
OT Eligibility: Applies to employees earning not more than SGD 2,600 basic per month (Part IV EA cap) or as configured per organisation.
4.4 Rest Day & Public Holiday Pay
Rest Day – Employee Request (EA s.37): Up to half day worked: employee paid half a day’s basic pay. Full day worked: employee paid one day’s basic pay.
Rest Day – Employer Request (EA s.37): Up to half day worked: employee paid one day’s basic pay. Full day worked: employee paid two days’ basic pay.
Public Holiday Work (EA s.88): Employee required to work on gazetted public holiday: paid normal salary for the day plus one additional day’s basic pay; alternatively, one day off-in-lieu by mutual agreement. Off-in-lieu is credited as a leave balance, not monetary payment.
Singapore Gazetted Public Holidays (11 per year): New Year’s Day, Chinese New Year (2 days), Good Friday, Labour Day, Hari Raya Puasa, National Day, Hari Raya Haji, Deepavali, Christmas Day. Plus any additional public holidays declared by the Government. System is pre-loaded and updated annually by vendor by 1 November for the following year.
4.5 CPF Contribution Rules
Ordinary Wages (OW): Wages due in the month: basic salary, fixed allowances, overtime pay. OW Ceiling: SGD 6,800 per month (effective 1 January 2025; subject to annual CPF Board review).
Additional Wages (AW): Wages not due every month: bonuses, AWS, commissions paid irregularly. AW Ceiling per year: SGD 102,000 minus total OW subject to CPF for the year.
CPF Formula: CPF Contribution = (OW subject to CPF × Rate) + (AW subject to CPF × Rate). OW subject to CPF = min(OW, OW Ceiling). AW subject to CPF = min(AW, AW Ceiling for the year).
Rounding: CPF contributions rounded to the nearest dollar. If cents ≥ 50 cents, round up; otherwise round down.
Submission Deadline: CPF contributions due by the 14th of the following month. Late payment attracts late payment interest of 1.5% per month from CPF Board. The below table needs to be able to modify and add new lines.

4.6 Skills Development Levy (SDL)
Applicability: SDL is payable by the employer for ALL employees (local and foreign) earning less than SGD 4,500 per month.
Rate: 0.25% of gross monthly remuneration.
Minimum: SGD 2.00 per employee per month.
Maximum: SGD 11.25 per employee per month (i.e., 0.25% of SGD 4,500 cap).
Formula: SDL = min(max(Gross Monthly Remuneration × 0.0025, SGD 2.00), SGD 11.25).
Submission: SDL is remitted together with CPF contributions via CPF Board’s e-Submit@cpf by the 14th of the following month. Payable to SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG).
Workflow: System auto-computes SDL for all eligible employees during payroll run. SDL amounts included in payroll journal entries. SDL submission file generated in CPF Board flat-file format. Confirmation receipt stored with payroll run record.
4.7 Foreign Worker Levy (FWL)
Applicability: FWL applies to Work Permit (WP) holders and S-Pass holders employed by the organisation.
Rate Determination: FWL rate varies by: (1) Sector (Services, Construction, Marine, Process, Manufacturing), (2) Worker tier (Basic Skilled vs Higher Skilled), (3) Dependency Ratio Ceiling (DRC) – percentage of foreign workers relative to total workforce.
Formula: Monthly FWL = Applicable Daily FWL Rate × Number of Days in Month.
Payment: FWL remitted to MOM by the 14th of the following month, together with CPF and SDL.
System Behaviour: HR Admin configures FWL rate table by pass type and sector. System auto-applies correct rate based on employee’s pass type. FWL amount shown in payroll journal and employer cost report. System alerts HR when DRC approaches the sector quota limit.
4.8 Payslip Requirements (MOM Mandatory)
Under the Employment Act, employers must issue an itemised payslip to every employee. The following fields are mandatory per MOM requirements:
	•	Full legal name and NRIC/FIN of employee
	•	Employer name and Unique Entity Number (UEN)
	•	Payslip date and salary payment date
	•	Start and end date of the salary period
	•	Basic salary for the period
	•	All allowances itemised separately (transport, housing, meal, etc.)
	•	Overtime hours worked and overtime pay amount
	•	All deductions itemised (employee CPF, salary advance recovery, absent deductions, etc.)
	•	Net salary payable (Gross Pay − All Deductions + Reimbursements)
	•	Year-to-date (YTD) employee CPF contributions
	•	Year-to-date (YTD) employer CPF contributions
Payslips are auto-generated as PDF upon payroll finalisation, published to employee ESS portal, and email notification sent. Payslips must be retained for 5 years per MOM record-keeping requirements.
4.9 Bank GIRO Payment File
Net Pay Formula: Net Pay = Gross Pay − Employee CPF − Employee Medisave (if applicable) − Salary Advances / Loan Repayments − Absence Deductions + Approved Reimbursements.
Supported Banks: DBS/POSB (IDEAL format), OCBC (Velocity format), UOB (BIBPlus format), Standard Chartered, HSBC, Citibank, Maybank. File format configurable per bank specification.
File Contents: Employer bank account, employee bank code, branch code, account number, employee name, payment amount, payment reference (payroll period).
Reconciliation: Bank returns acknowledgement file after processing. System reconciles payment status per employee; flags failed credits for HR follow-up.
4.10 CPF e-Submit & IRAS AIS Workflow
CPF e-Submit Monthly: After payroll finalisation, system generates CPF contribution flat-file (.txt) in CPF Board prescribed format. File fields: Employer UEN, contribution month, employee NRIC/FIN, employee name, OW amount, AW amount, employee CPF, employer CPF, employee Medisave amount. Payroll Officer uploads file to CPF e-Submit@cpf portal. Portal validates file and returns confirmation reference number, stored in HRMS audit log. CPF contributions due by 14th of following month.
IRAS AIS / IR8A (Annual): At year-end, system aggregates annual employment income per employee: employment income, director fees, bonuses (AW), benefits-in-kind (Appendix 8A), stock option gains (Appendix 8B), CPF contributions. System generates IR8A data file in IRAS-prescribed format. Submitted to IRAS via AIS e-Service portal by 1 March annually. IRAS confirmation receipt stored in HRMS.
IR21 Tax Clearance (Foreign Employees): Triggered automatically when offboarding is initiated for non-SC/non-PR employees. Employer must file IR21 at least 1 month before last day (or 1 week if sudden departure). System withholds all monies due to the employee until IRAS issues tax clearance. System tracks IR21 status (Submitted / Clearance Issued / Payment Released) and provides deadline reminders. IR21 auto-populated from payroll YTD data: employment income, benefits-in-kind, stock option gains.
Appendix 8A (Benefits in Kind): System computes annual value of all non-cash benefits using IRAS prescribed formulas: company car (IRAS prescribed value), housing (annual value per IRAS), club membership (actual cost), group insurance premium (if taxable). Appended to IR8A filing.
Appendix 8B (Stock Options / ESOP): Records gains from stock option exercise during the year. Employer reports: date of grant, date of exercise, number of shares, open market value at exercise, option price, taxable gains. Submitted to IRAS via AIS.

Contribution rates applied per CPF Board table: SC/PR 3+ yrs ≤55: Employee 20%, Employer 17%, Total 37%. Ages 55-60: 16% / 15% / 31%. Ages 60-65: 10.5% / 11.5% / 22%. Ages 65-70: 7.5% / 9% / 16.5%. Above 70: 5% / 7.5% / 12.5%.
PR Year 1 graduated: Employee 5%, Employer 4% (or full rates if jointly elected at CPF Board). PR Year 2: Employee 15%, Employer 9%.
Rounding: round up if cents ≥ 50, else round down. CPF rates must be updateable by Super Admin without code deployment (rate table in admin UI).
Employee CPF deducted from gross pay. Employer CPF recorded as liability in payroll journal. Both lines itemised on payslip.
Must Have
CPF Act Cap. 36
PAY-003
Gross Pay & OT Computation
Gross Pay = Basic Salary + All OW Allowances + Overtime Pay + Shift Differentials + AW Bonuses/Commission + Government-paid leave reimbursements − NPL Deductions − Absence Deductions.
OT Pay = Hourly Basic Rate × 1.5 × OT Hours. Hourly Basic Rate = (Monthly Basic × 12) ÷ (52 × Weekly Contracted Hours). Monthly OT cap: 72 hours (EA s.38). System blocks OT accrual at cap; HR override requires documented justification in audit trail.
Rest Day (Employee Request): 0.5 day worked = 0.5 day pay; full day = 1 day pay. Rest Day (Employer Request): 0.5 day worked = 1 day pay; full day = 2 days pay.
Public Holiday work: normal salary + 1 day’s basic pay; or 1 day off-in-lieu.
AWS = 1 month’s basic salary (industry norm). Pro-rated: AWS × (Months Served ÷ 12). Shift Differential = Basic Pay × Shift Premium Rate (e.g., 1.1 for night shift), applied to hours within shift window.
Must Have
EA Cap. 91 s.38
PAY-004
SDL & FWL Computation
SDL: computed for all employees earning < SGD 4,500/month. Formula: min(max(Gross Pay × 0.0025, SGD 2.00), SGD 11.25) per employee per month. Remitted with CPF by 14th of following month.
FWL: computed for WP and S-Pass holders. FWL Rate configured by HR Admin per sector (Services / Construction / Marine / Process / Manufacturing) and worker tier (Basic Skilled / Higher Skilled). Monthly FWL = Daily FWL Rate × Days in Month. Remitted to MOM by 14th of following month.
Both SDL and FWL amounts included in payroll journal entries. System generates combined CPF/SDL submission file and separate FWL report for MOM remittance.
Alerts: system warns HR when FWL dependency ratio ceiling (DRC) for a sector approaches the MOM-set quota limit.

5. Module 2: Leave Management
The leave module manages all statutory and company leave types across the full employee lifecycle. Entitlement rules, proration formulas, approval workflows, and government-paid leave tracking are all automated based on Singapore statutory requirements.
5.1 Singapore Leave Entitlements – Statutory Basis
Annual Leave (EA s.88A): Minimum entitlement: 7 days for the first year of service, increasing by 1 day per year up to a maximum of 14 days for 8+ years of service. Entitlement only accrues after 3 months of service. Pro-rated for new joiners and leavers (see proration formulas below). AL must be taken; any untaken AL that cannot be carried forward must be encashed upon cessation of employment.
Sick Leave (EA s.89): 14 days outpatient sick leave per year (after 6 months of service; pro-rated in first 6 months: 5 days at 3 months, 8 days at 4 months, 11 days at 5 months). Up to 60 days hospitalisation leave per year (inclusive of 14 days outpatient). Valid Medical Certificate (MC) or Memo from a Singapore-registered doctor required. Employee must inform manager on the first day of sick leave absence.
Maternity Leave (CDCA s.9): 16 weeks for SC or PR child (or child who becomes SC within 12 months of birth). 8 weeks for non-SC/non-PR child. First 8 weeks employer-paid; last 8 weeks (for SC/PR child) government-reimbursed via MSF at capped amount. Employee must have worked for employer for at least 3 months before confinement. Leave may be taken in one continuous stretch or flexibly within 12 months of birth if employer agrees.
Paternity Leave (CDCA s.12A): 2 weeks for SC or PR child. Government-reimbursed at capped daily rate. Must be taken within 16 weeks of child’s birth. Employee must be legally married. Minimum 3 months’ service before birth.
Childcare Leave (CDCA s.12): 6 days per year for parents of at least one SC child under 7 years of age. First 3 days employer-paid; next 3 days government-reimbursed. Applicable to both parents per year. Employee must have worked at least 3 months.
Extended Childcare Leave (CDCA s.12H): 2 days per year for parents of at least one SC child aged 7 to 12 years (inclusive). Government-reimbursed at capped rate.
Shared Parental Leave (CDCA s.12B): Up to 4 weeks transferred from mother’s maternity leave entitlement to father. Government-reimbursed. Must be used within 12 months of birth. Father must be legally married to child’s mother.
Infant Care Leave: 6 days per year for parents of SC child under 2 years of age. Government-reimbursed.
Unpaid Infant Care Leave (UICL): 6 days per year for parents of SC child under 2 years. Unpaid; employer must grant. Not reimbursed.
Adoption Leave (CDCA s.12G): 4 weeks for adoptive mother of SC child. Government-reimbursed. Taken within 1 year of formal adoption order.
NS Leave (Enlistment Act): Employer must grant leave for NS in-camp training or full-time NS. NS Make-Up Pay applies. No specific entitlement limit – as ordered by MINDEF/SCDF/SPF.
No-Pay Leave (EA s.88A): Unpaid leave by mutual agreement. Salary deduction = (Monthly Salary ÷ Working Days in Month) × NPL Days taken. CPF contributions reduced proportionally.
5.2 Leave Proration Formulas
New Joiner AL Proration: AL Entitled for current leave year = Full Year AL Entitlement × (Months Remaining in Leave Year ÷ 12). Months Remaining = number of complete months from join month to end of leave year + 1 if join date is on or before the 15th of the month. E.g., joins 10 March in a Jan–Dec leave year: 10 months remaining ÷ 12 = 83% of full year entitlement.
Resignee AL Proration: AL Entitled for final year = Full Year AL Entitlement × (Months Served in Final Leave Year ÷ 12). If Leave Taken exceeds Entitled: excess deducted from final salary at Daily Rate = Monthly Basic ÷ 26. If Leave Balance remains: Encashment Value = (Monthly Basic ÷ 26) × Remaining Leave Days.
Sick Leave Proration (first year): 3 months service: 5 days outpatient. 4 months: 8 days. 5 months: 11 days. From 6 months: full 14 days. System auto-applies based on length of service at date of each sick leave application.
5.3 Leave Carry-Forward Rules
Configuration: HR Admin configures per leave type: maximum carry-forward days (e.g., 14 days AL), expiry date for carried-forward leave (e.g., 31 March of following year), auto-forfeit rule for days exceeding carry-forward limit (forfeit or flag for encashment approval).
Year-End Processing: At end of leave year (31 December or service anniversary depending on configuration), system computes unused AL balance. Carries forward up to the configured maximum. Excess balance forfeited or flagged for encashment approval. Carried-forward balance tagged with expiry date.
Expiry Alert: Employee reminded 30 days before expiry of carried-forward leave via in-app notification and email.

8. Module 5: Time & Attendance
Hourly Basic Rate (for OT/rest day calculations): = (Monthly Basic Salary × 12) ÷ (52 × Weekly Contracted Hours). This rate excludes allowances unless contractually stated otherwise.
Daily Basic Rate (for deductions): = Monthly Basic Salary ÷ Working Days in Month. Working days per month = actual calendar working days in the month (excluding weekends and public holidays).
Absent Deduction Formula: Daily Deduction = Monthly Basic ÷ Working Days in Month. Total Deduction = Daily Deduction × Days Absent Without Approval.

TAT-003
OT, Rest Day & Public Holiday Computation
OT Calculation: OT Hours = max(0, Hours Worked − Contracted Daily Hours). Monthly OT cap: 72 hours (EA s.38). OT Pay = OT Hours × (Hourly Basic Rate × 1.5). System enforces 72-hour cap; alerts HR and employee when 60 hours reached; blocks further OT accrual at 72 hours unless HR overrides with documented justification.
Rest Day (Employee Request): 0.5 day worked = 0.5 × Daily Basic Rate paid. Full day worked = 1 × Daily Basic Rate paid. EA s.37(1).
Rest Day (Employer Request): 0.5 day worked = 1 × Daily Basic Rate paid. Full day worked = 2 × Daily Basic Rate paid. EA s.37(2).
Public Holiday Work (EA s.88): employee paid normal daily salary for the day + 1 additional day’s basic pay. Or: 1 day off-in-lieu by mutual agreement (credited as OIL leave balance).
All computed amounts passed automatically to payroll module. Payroll Officer sees attendance-linked payroll components in pre-approval review.

12. Module 9: Offboarding
12.1 Final Pay Computation (Employment Act)
Salary for Days Worked in Final Month: = (Monthly Salary ÷ Working Days in Month) × Days Worked in Final Month.
Salary-in-Lieu of Notice (EA s.11): = (Monthly Basic Salary ÷ 26) × Notice Period Days Not Served. Subject to CPF as OW.
Leave Encashment: = (Monthly Basic Salary ÷ 26) × Unused AL Days. Unused AL = Pro-rated AL Entitlement for the year − AL Days Taken YTD. CPF applicable on leave encashment (treated as AW).
Excess Leave Deduction: If Leave Taken > Pro-rated Entitlement: Deduction = (Monthly Basic ÷ 26) × Excess Leave Days.
Outstanding Claims: All approved claims included in final payroll as normal non-CPF reimbursements.
Salary Advance Recovery: Full outstanding balance of salary advances or staff loans deducted from final pay, subject to EA s.27 deduction limits (max 25% of monthly salary per deduction). If total deduction would exceed net pay, HR must agree repayment schedule.
CPF on Final Pay: CPF applicable on final month OW (including salary-in-lieu of notice, leave encashment). Termination payments (retrenchment benefits, ex-gratia) generally not subject to CPF. Final CPF included in final month’s CPF submission.

18. Assumptions & Constraints
18.1 Assumptions
	•	The organisation has a stable broadband internet connection (minimum 10 Mbps per concurrent user) suitable for cloud HRMS access.
	•	Existing employee data is available in structured format (Excel/CSV) for data migration. Vendor provides data migration template and validation tool.
	•	The vendor is responsible for maintaining statutory compliance updates (CPF Board rates, MOM Employment Act changes, IRAS format changes, SDL rates, FWL rates) as part of the subscription, at no additional charge.
	•	User Acceptance Testing (UAT) will be conducted by HR, Finance, and IT representatives for minimum 4 weeks before go-live.
	•	The organisation will designate at least one Super Admin and one HR Admin as primary system contacts during implementation.
	•	Integration with third-party systems (accounting software, biometric devices) is subject to separate scoping. Core HRMS go-live does not depend on integration completion.
	•	For government-paid leave reimbursement (GPML, GPPL, CCL, SPL): the organisation will submit claims to MSF/CPF Board through the prescribed government portals. HRMS provides the claim data and amounts.
18.2 Constraints
	•	All employee personal data must reside in Singapore or PDPA-approved jurisdictions under the Second Schedule of the PDPA.
	•	Implementation timeline and budget are subject to organisational approval processes.
	•	Third-party integrations are subject to existing IT security policies and approval by the organisation’s IT department.
	•	The HRMS must not require proprietary hardware beyond standard end-user devices (PC, laptop, smartphone).
	•	The system must comply with MAS Technology Risk Management Guidelines where the organisation is a regulated financial entity.
	•	CPF rates, IRAS formats, and MOM statutory requirements are set by Singapore government authorities and may change annually; the system must be updated to reflect changes before they take effect.
